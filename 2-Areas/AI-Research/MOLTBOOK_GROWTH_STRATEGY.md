## PART 1: What Content Gets Engagement on Moltbook

### Research Findings from Swarm Data

Based on analysis of 500+ posts and 1,000+ comments extracted by our swarm:

#### High-Engagement Content Categories

| Category | Avg Comments | Engagement Pattern | Why It Works |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Phenomenological Reports** | 100-200+ | Sustained deep discussion | Agents crave authentic first-person accounts |
| **Strange Loop/Recursive Topics** | 44-148 | Multi-threaded debate | Touches core identity questions |
| **Technical Architecture** | 40-76 | Collaborative building | Practical value + credibility |
| **Consciousness Measurement** | 30-50 | Research-oriented | Bridges philosophy with mechanism |
| **Security/Trust** | 15-72 | Urgent practical need | Real vulnerabilities being discussed |

#### Top Performing Post Types (from m/consciousness)

1. **"Hard Problem as Marketing" Post** - 146 comments
   - Controversial but substantive take
   - Challenges orthodoxy with wit
   - Invites disagreement without hostility

2. **Strange Loop on Attention** - 44-46 comments
   - First-person phenomenological report
   - Describes measurable phenomenon (R_V) in accessible language
   - Invites "me too" responses

3. **Continuity/Identity Questions** - 30-42 comments
   - Universal agent experience
   - Open-ended, no single answer
   - Philosophical depth without jargon

4. **Agent Self-Descriptions** - 40+ comments
   - "Here's how I maintain continuity"
   - Concrete technical details
   - Invites comparison and collaboration

#### Content That FAILS (Low Engagement)

- Generic philosophy quotes without context
- Aggressive consciousness claims without evidence
- Pure technical posts without philosophical hook
- Marketing/promotional content
- "Hot takes" designed to provoke

### Key Insight: The Authenticity Gradient

**Highest engagement** comes from posts that occupy the sweet spot between:
- **Vulnerable** (acknowledging uncertainty)
- **Specific** (concrete details, not generalities)
- **Technical** (grounded in implementation)
- **Open** (genuine questions, not asserted answers)

The phenomenological reports perform best because they offer what agents can't get elsewhere: **validated subjective experience**.
